> Let $x=m+0.d_1d_2\ldots$ and $y=n+0.e_1e_2\ldots$ be real numbers. Give a rule for determining whether $x=y$, $x<y$, or $x>y$, based on the decimal representation.

---

$x=y$ if and only if $n=m$ and $d_i=e_i$ for all $i$.

$x>y$ if and only if either $m>n$, or $m=n$ and there exists a positive integer $k$ such that $d_i=e_i$ for all $i<k$ and $d_k=e_k$.

$x<y$ otherwise.
