> Without using the $\Sigma$-notation, write out the equivalent of
>
> $$ \sum_{0\le n\le5}\frac1{2n+1}, $$
>
> and also the equivalent of
>
> $$ \sum_{0\le n^2\le5}\frac1{2n^2+1}. $$

---

The set of the integer values of $n$ that satisfy the condition $0\le n\le5$ is $\\{0,1,2,3,4,5\\}$, so

$$
\begin{align}
\sum_{0\le n\le5}\frac1{2n+1} &= \frac1{2\cdot0+1}+\frac1{2\cdot1+1}+\frac1{2\cdot2+1}+\frac1{2\cdot3+1}+\frac1{2\cdot4+1}+\frac1{2\cdot5+1} \\
                              &= \frac1{0+1}+\frac1{2+1}+\frac1{4+1}+\frac1{6+1}+\frac1{8+1}+\frac1{10+1} \\
                              &= \frac11+\frac13+\frac15+\frac17+\frac19+\frac1{11}. \\
\end{align}
$$

The set of the integer values of $n$ that satisfy the condition $0\le n^2\le5$ is $\\{-2,-1,0,1,2\\}$, so

$$
\begin{align}
\sum_{0\le n^2\le5}\frac1{2n^2+1} &= \frac1{2\cdot(-2)^2+1}+\frac1{2\cdot(-1)^2+1}+\frac1{2\cdot0^2+1}+\frac1{2\cdot1^2+1}+\frac1{2\cdot2^2+1} \\
                                  &= \frac1{2\cdot4+1}+\frac1{2\cdot1+1}+\frac1{2\cdot0+1}+\frac1{2\cdot1+1}+\frac1{2\cdot4+1} \\
                                  &= \frac1{8+1}+\frac1{2+1}+\frac1{0+1}+\frac1{2+1}+\frac1{8+1} \\
                                  &= \frac19+\frac13+\frac11+\frac13+\frac19.
\end{align}
$$
